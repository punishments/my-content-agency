## Section 1 — Idea Summary

**What is this content about?**
Episode 1 of the Normal Never Wins series. Case study: the Key West Butterfly Conservatory stamps departing visitors with 3D butterfly stickers — a tactile, unexpected exit touchpoint that costs nothing and generates organic word-of-mouth. The insight extracted: most brands fight for attention at the entrance; this place won at the exit. The exit moment is the most underused marketing touchpoint. Application: what's your exit sticker?

**Source:** Research / case study — Normal Never Wins series

---

## Section 2 — Creative Strategist POV

**Angle 1: The Exit Moment as Underused Touchpoint**
- Direction: Most marketing thinking is front-loaded — acquisition, first impression, hooks. This case study proves the opposite: what you leave someone with is what they carry. The butterfly sticker is the proof; the exit moment principle is the content.
- Why it's differentiated: Not "unusual marketing works" (generic) — the specific mechanism (exit > entrance for memorability) is a usable insight, not just an entertaining anecdote.
- Audience resonance: Speaks to creators and professionals who think about audience retention, impressions, and standing out — without requiring them to be marketers.
- Brand fit: Skill pillar (marketing). Position mission — establishes a specific POV on an underrepresented idea.

**Creative Strategist recommendation:** Angle 1 — the exit moment insight is what makes this Position-worthy, not just the stunt.

## Section 4 — Content Producer Output

### Story Structure

**Problem:**
Most brands invest everything at the entrance — the first impression, the ad, the hook. The exit is an afterthought.

**Unconventional Pursue:**
The Key West Butterfly Conservatory stamps departing visitors with a 3D butterfly sticker — tactile, unexpected, personal. Guests walk out as a moving conversation piece. Zero ad spend.

**Payoff:**
The exit moment is the most underused touchpoint in any experience. What you leave someone with is what they carry — into every conversation after they leave.

**Application:**
What's your exit sticker? What do people leave your content carrying?

---

### Hooks

**[Framework — Reframe]**
"Everyone fights for attention at the entrance. The Key West Butterfly Conservatory won at the exit."

**[Adaptive — POV]**
"POV: You're leaving a butterfly conservatory. Someone presses a 3D butterfly sticker onto your jacket. You look down. You smile. You tell three people by dinner."

**Producer notes:**
The story-first spoken hook is the right call for Talking Head — it opens with the conservatory moment before any analysis. The reframe hook works as a caption opener. POV hook adapted for spoken delivery in the script.
