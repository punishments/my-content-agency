# Execution System

How the content operation runs day to day. Rules, constraints, and non-negotiables.

---

## Current Stage

**Account stage:** <10K followers
**Primary KPI:** Views
**Strategic priority:** Reach, repeatability, execution quality
**Format priority:** Mastery — not variety

This is a format mastery stage. The goal is to execute two formats cleanly and consistently, not to experiment with new ones. Every rep builds skill. Inconsistency resets it.

---

## Active Formats

### Talking Formats
Used for: trust-building, authority, personal connection, emotional delivery

| Format type | What it is | Intensity |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| **Talking head** | Direct to camera, single or multi-angle, spoken delivery | Low (single angle) / High (multi-angle) |
| **Voiceover** | Audio narration over footage or visuals — no direct camera | High |

**Minimum per week:** 1 talking post (target: 3)

### Non-Talking Formats
Used for: views, reach, shareability, low-friction consumption

| Format type | What it is | Intensity |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| **Silent film / B-roll + text** | Footage with on-screen text overlays — no spoken audio | Low |

**Minimum per week:** 1 non-talking post (target: 3)

---

## Format Rules

**Do not over-experiment.**
At this stage, introducing new formats dilutes execution quality. The two format categories above are the active formats. New formats are only introduced when there is a clear, justified strategic reason — not curiosity, not a trend.

**Format mastery comes before format variety.**
Executing one talking format and one non-talking format consistently is more valuable than producing six different formats inconsistently. Master the rep before adding complexity.

**Account data does not drive format decisions.**
Early account performance is noisy. A non-talking post that outperformed a talking post in week 2 is not a signal to stop making talking posts. Format strategy comes from 7x7 research and defined frameworks — not from individual post performance at this stage.

## Weekly Cadence

Every week, 6 posts must be planned, approved, scripted, and published.

| Dimension | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Total posts | 6 |
| Attract | 4 |
| Nurture | 1 |
| Position | 1 |
| Convert | 0 feed posts (stories/DMs are valid for Convert at this stage but outside this cadence) |
| Talking | 3 |
| Non-talking | 3 |
| High intensity | 2 |
| Low intensity | 4 |

If the week cannot hit 6 posts for a legitimate reason, the Marketing Director documents it. If it drops without documentation, it is a process failure.

## Weekly Rhythm

| Day | Activity |
|-----|---------|
| Monday | Run Content Decision System — generate ideas, assign to weekly plan |
| Monday–Tuesday | Run pipeline Steps 1–5 for each post (proposal stage) |
| Tuesday | User reviews and approves proposals |
| Wednesday | Copywriter writes approved copy |
| Wednesday–Thursday | QA Editor reviews all copy |
| Wednesday | Non-talking post published |
| Thursday–Friday | Film talking content |
| Friday | Talking post published |
| Weekend | Review analytics, update performance log |

The sequence is fixed. The specific days are flexible.

---

## The Pipeline Is Not Optional

Every piece of content — regardless of how simple or obvious the idea seems — runs through the full 10-step pipeline defined in `docs/content_pipeline.md`.

**Non-negotiables:**
- No script is written before Step 6 user approval
- No content is published without Step 8 QA sign-off
- No step is skipped

Process consistency is how quality consistency happens. A shortcut in the pipeline is not a time-saving — it is a quality trade-off.

---

## Account Data Rules

Account performance data is operational data. It tells you what happened. It does not tell you what to do next.

**Use account data for:**
- Baseline views and reach tracking
- Distribution actuals vs. targets
- Execution review (was the pipeline followed, were posts on schedule)

**Do NOT use account data for:**
- Identifying hook patterns
- Making format recommendations
- Informing creative strategy
- Drawing conclusions about what "works"

At <10K followers with limited post history, performance data is too noisy to be strategic. Patterns take 4+ weeks of consistent output to become readable. Until then, strategy comes from 7x7 research and defined frameworks.

---

## Cadence Enforcement

Cadence is tracked by the Content Strategist (Step 3) and verified by the Creative Director (Step 5).

**If a proposed assignment breaks weekly balance:**
1. Content Strategist flags it before submitting
2. An alternative assignment is made that maintains balance
3. If the week genuinely cannot be balanced, escalate to Marketing Director

**Common failures to prevent:**
- All 4 Attract slots filled before the week is halfway through
- Both High intensity slots used by Tuesday
- 3 Talking posts scheduled with no Non-talking planned
- Nurture or Position slot left unfilled until Friday

---

## Research Integration

A 7x7 research cycle runs every 5 weeks. Research informs:
- Hook library (`/research/hook_library.md`)
- Format library (`/research/format_library.md`)
- Pattern bank (`/research/patterns.md`)
- Content ideas added to the backlog

Research does NOT replace the pipeline, change cadence targets, or allow skipping the brand check.

Between cycles, add individual posts to the research database as you encounter them. Synthesis happens during the full cycle.

---

## Escalation Protocol

| Issue | Who handles it |
|-------|---------------|
| Cadence violation | Content Strategist flags → Marketing Director resolves |
| Script written before approval | Marketing Director flags as process violation |
| Post published without QA | Marketing Director flags and logs |
| Story structure too weak to approve | Creative Director returns to Content Producer |
| QA failure requiring full rewrite | QA Editor returns to Scriptwriter |
| Week cannot hit 6 posts | Marketing Director documents and adjusts |

---

## What "Done" Means

A piece of content is done when:
- [ ] Proposal approved by user
- [ ] Script written and saved to `/scripts/`
- [ ] QA sign-off received
- [ ] Published with caption and on-screen text as scripted
- [ ] Analytics stub created in `/analytics/raw/`
- [ ] Weekly plan updated with published status

A piece that is "mostly done" is not done.
