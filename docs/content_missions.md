# Content Missions

Every piece of content serves exactly one mission. Classify before you script.

---

## The Four Missions

### 1. Attract
**Goal:** Grow the audience. Bring new people in.

**What it does:** Uses story, emotion, or a relatable moment to earn attention from people who don't know you yet.

**Structure:** Hook → Relatable setup → Payoff insight or transformation moment

**Tone:** Open, inviting, human. The door is open.

**Payoff:** Yes — always end with a clear emotional resolution or insight. This is what makes it shareable.

**Examples:**
- "I spent 3 years in a job that was slowly making me smaller"
- "Nobody told me that overthinking was a self-protection strategy"
- A real moment of failure → turning point → result

**Signal words:** Story-first, emotional, broadly relatable, shareable, no product pitch

---

### 2. Nurture
**Goal:** Deepen connection with existing audience. Build trust and loyalty.

**What it does:** Shows the real person behind the content. Behind-the-scenes, process, day-in-the-life, honest updates.

**Structure:** Reveal → Reflection → What it means (optional)

**Tone:** Intimate, unpolished, honest. This is the locker room, not the stage.

**Payoff:** No hard payoff needed — the connection IS the value.

**Examples:**
- "Here's what my week actually looked like"
- "I'm figuring this out in real time too"
- A gym session, a travel moment, a reflection mid-process

**Signal words:** Behind the scenes, real talk, update, honest, process

---

### 3. Position
**Goal:** Establish authority and perspective. Be known for something specific.

**What it does:** Teaches a concept, shares a framework, or takes a clear point of view on a topic. Makes people say "they really know their stuff."

**Structure:** Insight → Framework or breakdown → Why it matters

**Tone:** Clear, confident, analytical. The expert in the room — without announcing it.

**Payoff:** Yes — leave them with something they can apply or think about differently.

**Examples:**
- "Here's how I approach career transitions using the bridge principle"
- "Why most people's communication fails at the listener level, not the speaker level"
- A breakdown of a system, method, or mental model

**Signal words:** Framework, breakdown, how-to, reframe, deeper look, insight

---

### 4. Convert
**Goal:** Drive action. Generate revenue, leads, or direct entry into the offer funnel.

**What it does:** A feed post — not a story, not a DM. Uses the best format and highest-value content to earn the ask. Everything before the CTA must stand alone as valuable content.

**Structure:** Pain → Solution → Proof (optional) → CTA

**Tone:** Direct, specific, confident. No apology, no soft-sell.

**Payoff:** Yes — the CTA IS the payoff.

**Format requirement:** Best format available. Highest-performing content topics. This is not a low-effort post with a link attached — it must earn the conversion.

**CTA types:** Buy, apply, book, download, enter lead funnel (link in bio, DM for info, etc.)

**Examples:**
- "If you're doing X, here's what I built to solve it — [link]"
- "I'm taking 5 people through [offer] next month"
- A tight testimonial → result → direct offer

**Signal words:** Join, download, apply, book, link in bio

**Current stage note:** Feed post target is 0 at <10K followers. Stories and DMs are valid Convert channels at this stage and fine to use — they're just not part of this cadence. Convert feed posts come later, when the audience and trust base support them.

---

## Mission Ratio (Suggested Starting Point)

| Mission | Frequency |
|---------|-----------|
| Attract | 40% |
| Nurture | 30% |
| Position | 20% |
| Convert | 10% |

Adjust based on funnel stage. Early audience = more Attract + Nurture. Established audience = more Position + Convert.

---

## Quick Classification Guide

Ask: **What do I want someone to do or feel after watching this?**

- Feel seen / share it → **Attract**
- Feel closer to me → **Nurture**
- Think differently / learn something → **Position**
- Take action / buy / sign up → **Convert**
