# Content Decision System

Idea extraction tool. Answers the question: where do this week's ideas come from?

This doc covers the extraction questions and personal triage filter. The full weekly session process — including how to run 6 proposals in sequence and confirm weekly balance — is in `docs/weekly_idea_session.md`.

---

## When to Use

Run every Monday during the weekly idea session (Step 2 of `docs/weekly_idea_session.md`) to generate raw ideas before triage and routing begins.

Also useful mid-week when a raw idea surfaces and you want to capture it cleanly before logging to `inputs/idea_dump.md`.

---

## Step 1 — Idea Extraction

Three questions. Write fast. Don't filter yet — capture everything.

**Question 1: What did I experience this week?**
A moment, decision, conversation, or observation that stuck with you. Something that surprised, frustrated, or clarified something.
→ Even a small moment counts. You don't need a dramatic story — you need a real one.

**Question 2: What did I learn, relearn, or finally understand?**
A shift in thinking. Something you used to believe that you no longer do. Something that clicked after years of not clicking.
→ This is often a Position idea. The moment of understanding is the content.

**Question 3: What am I currently struggling with — or just got through?**
Something that's unresolved, uncomfortable, or that you recently navigated. The struggle in progress is often more relatable than the victory.
→ This is often an Attract idea. Shared struggle creates recognition.

**Capture each answer in one sentence.** Don't develop it. Don't pitch it. Just state it plainly.

---

## Step 2 — Backlog and Idea Dump Check

Before triaging new ideas, check what's already waiting:

- Open `planning/content_backlog.md` — are there any High priority ideas ready to schedule this week?
- Open `inputs/idea_dump.md` — are there ideas captured since last Monday that haven't been assessed yet?

Pull these into the pool before triaging new ideas. Backlog items have already passed brand check — they go to triage directly.

---

## Step 3 — Quick Brand Check

For each new idea, one question before triage:

**Does this connect to a belief, pillar, or real audience pain?**

| Pillars | Beliefs | Audience pains |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| Skill | Success isn't linear | Overthinking |
| Mindset | Normal never wins | Feeling behind |
| Lifestyle | Growth requires discomfort | Invisible at work |
| Passions | The quiet ones often have the most to say | Stuck but unsure why |

If the idea connects clearly → move to triage.
If it doesn't connect to any pillar, belief, or pain → drop it or park it in the dump for later.

This is a 30-second check, not a deep analysis. If you can't see the connection in 30 seconds, it's probably not there.

---

## Step 4 — Triage Filter

For each idea that passed the brand check, run the 3-question triage from `docs/weekly_idea_session.md`:

| Question | Pass | Fail |
|----------|------|------|
| Genuine brand angle? | Proceed | Backlog or drop |
| Audience would stop scrolling? | Proceed | Sharpen or drop |
| Material + energy to execute this week? | Proceed | Backlog |

2 minutes per idea. If you're unsure after 2 minutes, it's not ready — park it.

---

## Step 5 — Output

After extraction and triage, you should have enough raw ideas to fill 6 slots (plus 2–3 extras for flexibility).

For each idea that passed triage, capture:

```
Idea: [One sentence — the raw idea]
Source: [Experience / Learning / Struggle / Backlog]
Connects to: [Pillar or belief or audience pain]
Triage: [PASS / BACKLOG / DROP]
```

Hand this list to the weekly idea session (Step 4 of `docs/weekly_idea_session.md`) for routing and slot assignment.

---

## Rules

- **Generate first, filter second.** Don't try to make ideas "good" during extraction. Capture everything, then triage.
- **Specificity beats volume.** One idea with a clear brand angle and a real moment behind it is worth more than five vague ones.
- **Struggle and process are underused.** Most creators default to lessons and advice. The in-progress struggle is almost always more relatable.
- **The brand check is not a vibe check.** It must connect to a pillar, belief, or audience pain — not just "feel on-brand."
