# Department Goals

Strategic goals for the content operation. Updated per 6-month cycle.

---

## Account Stage

**Current:** <10K followers
**Primary KPI:** Views
**Strategic focus:** Reach, repeatability, execution quality

---

## Data Usage Rule

At this stage:
- **7x7 research** → pattern recognition and creative strategy
- **Account data** → reporting and execution review only

Do not use account performance data to make strategic decisions until the account has a meaningful baseline (minimum 3 months of consistent output). Early data is noisy. Build the system first. Let data inform it later.

## Mission Distribution Logic

At this account stage, the distribution is designed to:

**4x Attract** — Primary growth driver. Most people who will find this account don't know it exists yet. Attract content is how they arrive.

**1x Nurture** — Trust anchor. The people who do find the account need a reason to stay. Nurture content rewards existing audience and signals realness.

**1x Position** — Authority signal. Even at small scale, establishing a clear point of view builds the long-term brand. One per week is enough to layer in without over-indexing on authority too early.

**0x Convert (feed)** — Feed post target is 0 at this stage. Stories and DMs are fine for Convert activity and not tracked here. Convert feed posts require best format + highest-value content + direct CTA to buy or enter the lead funnel — introduce when the account stage warrants it.

---

## Format Distribution Logic

**3x Talking** — Builds trust, authority, and personal connection. The viewer gets to know the creator's voice, thinking, and presence.

**3x Non-talking** — Drives views and reach. Lower friction to consume and share. More accessible to cold audiences.

Even split ensures neither trust nor reach is sacrificed.

---

## Intensity Distribution Logic

**4x Low** — The pipeline only sustains 6 posts per week if most posts are executable quickly. Low-intensity posts (under 1 hour) keep momentum without burning out.

**2x High** — Deep content signals craft and earns attention. But more than 2 per week is unsustainable at this stage and risks inconsistency.

---

## Success at 6 Months

The goal at the end of this cycle is not a follower number. It is:

1. A repeatable weekly execution habit that doesn't depend on motivation
2. A clear format signature — one talking format and one non-talking format we own
3. A hook library and pattern bank that gets richer with each research cycle
4. A content pipeline that runs cleanly without shortcuts
5. A growing view baseline that we can start using as strategic data

The follower count is a byproduct of those five things. Focus on them.
