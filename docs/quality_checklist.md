# Content Quality Checklist

Run this before finalizing any script. This is the creator-facing review — a fast check to confirm the script is ready before it goes to QA or before you give your Step 6 approval.

For the full format-specific QA review (run by the QA Editor at Step 8), see `docs/content_checklist.md`.

---

## Layer 1 — Story Structure

- [ ] **Problem is specific.** The opening surfaces a real internal struggle. A cold audience would think: "yes, that's exactly it." Not vague — not "sometimes we feel stuck."
- [ ] **Pursue is grounded.** The middle section is a turning point or realization rooted in real experience — not advice, not instruction. Something actually happened or shifted.
- [ ] **Payoff lands somewhere new.** The ending gives the viewer something to take with them. It is not a summary of what was said. It reframes or resolves.
- [ ] **No section is missing.** Problem → Pursue → Payoff. All three present.
- [ ] **No tangents.** Every line serves the arc. Nothing that belongs in a different piece.

---

## Layer 2 — Hook

- [ ] **First 3 seconds are clear.** A cold audience can understand the hook without context.
- [ ] **Self-recognition is immediate.** The viewer knows within 3 seconds whether this is for them.
- [ ] **Hook is specific enough.** "Smart people stay invisible" is specific. "Are you holding yourself back?" is not.
- [ ] **Hook is honest.** No manufactured urgency. No exaggeration.
- [ ] **Hook matches the story.** The hook promises something the content actually delivers.

---

## Layer 3 — Voice

- [ ] **Grounded.** Rooted in real experience, not theory or advice.
- [ ] **Honest.** Says the uncomfortable thing plainly. No softening.
- [ ] **Specific.** Every line is specific to this audience and this angle.
- [ ] **No hype.** No "game-changing," "life-transforming," or forced excitement.
- [ ] **Sounds like a person talking.** Read it out loud. If it sounds like a blog post, rewrite it.

---

## Final Pass — 3 Questions

Answer these before approving or signing off:

1. **Self-recognition:** Would someone in the target audience say "this is me" in the first 3 seconds?
2. **Story clarity:** Is the arc (Problem → Pursue → Payoff) immediately clear without effort?
3. **Format clarity:** Does the format (talking or non-talking) match how the content is written?

Any NO = identify what needs to change before proceeding.

---

## Where This Fits in the Pipeline

| When | Who | What |
|------|-----|------|
| Step 6 — before approving a proposal | You (the creator) | Use Layer 2 (Hook) and a quick story check to evaluate the proposal's story structure |
| Step 8 — before signing off on a script | QA Editor | Runs the full `docs/content_checklist.md` — all layers, all format checks |
| Before filming | You (the creator) | Run this full checklist on the approved script one final time |

A script that passes this checklist is ready to film. A script that doesn't should go back to the Scriptwriter or QA Editor — not to the camera.
