# 7x7 Research Workflow

Standard operating procedure for a full research cycle. Follow this in order. Do not skip steps.

---

## Step 1 — Choose Creators

**Goal:** Select 7 creators worth studying for this cycle.

**How to choose:**
- Start with creators whose content you've already noticed performing well in your feed or niche
- Look for at least one creator with a follower count lower than their content quality would suggest
- Ensure variety across: content style (emotional vs. analytical), format (talking head vs. silent), audience type (career, lifestyle, self-development)
- Avoid: creators in your exact niche doing the same thing — you want adjacent angles, not mirrors

**Output:** A list of 7 creator handles with one sentence on why each was chosen.

**Where to save:** New file at `/research/[cycle-name]_database.md` using the database template.

---

## Step 2 — Gather Posts

**Goal:** For each creator, collect 7 posts that overperformed their average.

**How to find outliers:**
- Go to their profile and sort by most viewed or most shared if possible
- Look for posts where the view count is visibly higher than their surrounding content
- Check comments for volume and emotional intensity — high comment velocity is a signal
- Prioritize shares over views when both are visible

**What to collect per post:**
- Direct link
- View count and share count
- A rough note on what caught your attention

**Do not analyze yet.** Capture first, analyze after. Mixing the two slows you down and biases your observations.

**Output:** 7 links per creator with raw metrics noted.

---

## Step 3 — Identify True Outliers

**Goal:** Confirm which posts are genuinely worth deep analysis.

**Filter each post through:**
- Is this 3x+ their average view count, OR does it have unusually high shares relative to views?
- Does the comment section show genuine emotional response — not just "great video"?
- Would the average person in your audience stop scrolling for this?

**If a post doesn't pass:** Swap it for a different one. Quality of outlier > filling the quota.

**Output:** A confirmed list of 7 posts per creator ready for analysis.

---

## Step 4 — Analyze Each Post

**Goal:** Deconstruct each post using the full field set from the database template.

**For each post, document:**

1. **Hook breakdown** — spoken hook, text hook, visual hook. Write these out exactly as they appear.
2. **Format** — name the format type specifically. "Talking head with cuts every 3s" is better than just "talking head."
3. **Structure** — what narrative arc does it follow? (pain → payoff, open loop, contrarian setup, internal dialogue, etc.)
4. **Emotional trigger** — what does the viewer *feel* in the first 3 seconds? Name it precisely.
5. **Funnel stage** — is this designed to attract new viewers, nurture existing ones, build authority, or convert?
6. **CTA** — exact text, placement (mid / end), tone (warm / direct / none).
7. **Why it worked** — one paragraph. Focus on the mechanism, not the topic. "It worked because..." not "It was about..."
8. **What I can adapt** — one to three sentences on how to apply the mechanics to your own content and audience.

**Rule:** "Why it worked" must describe a mechanism. If your answer could apply to any video, it's not specific enough.

**Output:** Completed database entry for all 7 posts per creator.

---

## Step 5 — Synthesize Patterns

**Goal:** After all 49 posts are analyzed, step back and find what repeats.

**Use the analysis template at `/research/7x7_analysis_template.md`.**

**Look for:**
- Hook patterns that appear across multiple creators — these are universal signals, not individual quirks
- Format mechanics that show up in the highest-performing posts specifically
- Emotional triggers that appear in posts with high share rates (not just views)
- Funnel patterns — which missions are getting the most reach? Where are creators leaving CTA gaps?
- Structures that appear more than twice across different creators

**How to synthesize:**
- Group similar observations, don't list them individually
- Look for the pattern underneath the pattern — "POV hooks" is a category, but "POV hooks that put the viewer inside an anxiety they already have" is the actual mechanism
- Note anything that surprises you — surprise is a signal of a pattern you haven't used yet

**Output:** Completed analysis template with sections filled in.

---

## Step 6 — Save Insights

**Goal:** Convert your synthesis into reusable assets.

**For each insight, decide where it lives:**

| Insight type | Where it goes |
|-------------|--------------|
| A new hook pattern | `/research/hook_library.md` |
| A format mechanic you want to test | `/research/format_library.md` |
| A storytelling structure | `/research/patterns.md` |
| An emotional trigger worth activating | `/research/patterns.md` |
| A CTA pattern | `/research/patterns.md` |

**Format for each entry:**
- Pattern name
- One real example from the research
- Why it works (mechanism)
- How to apply it to your brand specifically

**Do not add an insight to the library if you can't write the "how to apply it to your brand" section.** An observation without an application is just a note, not an asset.

---

## Step 7 — Turn Insights into Content Ideas

**Goal:** Exit the research cycle with a list of testable content ideas.

**For each strong pattern you identified, generate at least one content idea using this format:**

```
Concept: [one-line description]
Hook angle: [which hook pattern to use]
Format: [which format]
Structure: [which narrative structure]
Mission: [Attract / Nurture / Position / Convert]
Pillar: [Skill / Mindset / Lifestyle / Passions]
What makes it mine: [one sentence on how this is specific to your voice + audience]
```

**Add these to `/planning/content_backlog.md`** with priority and target week.

**Minimum output per research cycle:** 5 content ideas ready to script.

---

## Cycle Cadence

Run a full 7x7 cycle every 4–6 weeks. Between cycles, add individual posts to the database as you encounter them organically. A research cycle is not the only time you can observe — but it is the only time you synthesize.
