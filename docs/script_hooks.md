# Script Hook Framework

Hook templates for short-form scripts. Used in all script generation.
Referenced by `docs/scripting_prompt.md`.

Every script gets 4 hook options: **2 framework + 2 adaptive.**

---

## Framework Hooks

Framework hooks are structural templates. The formula stays constant — only the topic changes. They work reliably across different content types because the mechanics are proven.

---

### Framework Hook 1 — Self-Recognition Hook

**What it does:** Names exactly who the viewer is and something specific they do or think — before they have a chance to decide whether to watch.

**Formula:**
> If you've ever [specific behavior or thought the audience has but doesn't say out loud] — this is for you.

**Or:**
> [Identity marker]. [Private thought or behavior they recognize but haven't said aloud.]

**Examples:**
- "If you've ever had five tabs open on what to do with your career and closed them all without reading — this is for you."
- "Ambitious. Overthinking. And somehow still not moving. That's the thing no one talks about."
- "You're not lazy. You know exactly what you want. You just can't make yourself start."

**When to use:** Any mission. Strongest for Attract and Nurture.
**Signal this creates:** "That's me." Immediate recognition → retention.
**Avoid:** Making it too broad ("if you've ever felt stuck"). The more specific the behavior, the stronger the recognition.

---

### Framework Hook 2 — Reframe Hook

**What it does:** States a widely accepted belief, then immediately challenges it. Creates productive friction — the viewer stays to find out why they've been thinking about it wrong.

**Formula:**
> Everyone thinks [accepted belief]. The real issue is [underlying truth they haven't considered].

**Or:**
> It's not [what people assume]. It's [what's actually true].

**Examples:**
- "Everyone thinks overthinking is a focus problem. It's actually a trust problem."
- "It's not that you don't know what to do. It's that you don't believe it'll work for you."
- "The thing stopping you isn't discipline. It's the story you have about what happens if you fail."
- "Everyone talks about building habits. Nobody talks about what to do when the habit breaks."

**When to use:** Position and Attract content. Strong for topics where conventional wisdom is incomplete.
**Signal this creates:** Friction → curiosity → "okay, explain." Viewer has to stay to resolve the tension.
**Avoid:** Using a belief that isn't actually widely held. The contrast only works if the "wrong" belief is real.

## Adaptive Hooks

Adaptive hooks are built from the specific topic, viewer, and moment. They don't follow a fixed formula — they adapt to context. Use these when the topic has a natural POV moment, internal voice, or timing-specific angle.

---

### Adaptive Hook Type 1 — POV Hook

**What it does:** Places the viewer inside a specific moment they know intimately. The POV frame creates instant immersion — the viewer isn't watching someone else, they're watching themselves.

**Formula:**
> POV: [identity] + [specific recurring moment or situation]

**Examples:**
- "POV: you've been 'almost ready to start' for three weeks"
- "POV: person who has a plan for every scenario except actually executing"
- "POV: you gave someone else advice today that you haven't taken yourself"
- "POV: you know what you need to do. You just keep thinking about it instead."
- "POV: first time someone actually paid you for the thing you're good at"

**When to use:** Attract and Nurture content. Lifestyle and Mindset pillars. Any moment with a strong visual or emotional context.
**Signal this creates:** "I've been in that exact moment." Viewer feels seen without being spoken to directly.
**Adapt by:** Making the moment as specific as possible. Generic POV hooks don't stop scrolls.

---

### Adaptive Hook Type 2 — Internal Voice Hook

**What it does:** Externalizes the viewer's inner saboteur or internal monologue — the thoughts they carry privately. The hook gives voice to something they've never heard said out loud, which creates immediate relief and recognition.

**Formula:**
> 🧠: [exact saboteur thought — precise, private, slightly uncomfortable]

**Or without emoji:**
> "That voice that tells you [specific thought]? Here's what's underneath it."

**Examples:**
- "🧠: you're too late to start"
- "🧠: everyone else has it figured out except you"
- "🧠: if you were actually good at this, it wouldn't feel this hard"
- "🧠: don't post it, no one's going to watch"
- "🧠: you should have started this three years ago"

**When to use:** Nurture and Attract content. Mindset pillar. Strongest for overthinking, self-doubt, execution gap topics.
**Signal this creates:** "How did they know that?" Immediate intimacy — viewer feels understood before a single piece of advice is given.
**Adapt by:** Making the thought as specific and private as possible. The more it sounds like something someone would be embarrassed to admit, the harder it lands.

## Hook Selection Guide

| Content mission | Best hook type | Why |
|----------------|---------------|-----|
| Attract | Reframe or POV | Stops cold scroll; creates curiosity or recognition |
| Nurture | Self-Recognition or Internal Voice | Builds intimacy; rewards existing audience |
| Position | Reframe | Establishes authority through a specific point of view |
| Convert | Self-Recognition | Earns trust before making an ask |

---

## Hook Quality Test

Before selecting a hook for a final script, run it through:

1. **Cold audience test** — Would someone who has never seen your content stop for this? Read it as a stranger.
2. **Specificity test** — Could this hook belong to a generic account? If yes, make it more specific.
3. **3-second test** — Can it be read or heard in under 3 seconds? If not, cut it.
4. **Recognition test** — Does it name a feeling, thought, or behavior the viewer already has? If it introduces something new, it's not a hook — it's a setup.

## Hook Writing Shortcuts

When stuck, use these as starting points:

**For internal struggle topics:**
> "You're not [negative label]. You're [true diagnosis]."

**For execution gap topics:**
> "The problem isn't [surface issue]. It's [real issue one level deeper]."

**For identity topics:**
> "Nobody talks about what it's actually like to be [identity] and [contradicting state]."

**For career/communication topics:**
> "The thing I wish I knew before [specific situation]."

**For lifestyle/behind-the-scenes:**
> "I'm not going to [expected behavior]. Here's what's actually happening."
