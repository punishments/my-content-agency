# 7x7 Research Method

## What It Is

A structured competitive intelligence method. You study 7 creators and 7 outlier posts per creator to extract repeatable patterns — not to copy ideas, but to understand the mechanisms behind content that performs.

The output is not "interesting observations." The output is a reusable library of hooks, formats, structures, and triggers that you adapt to your own voice.

---

## Why This Method

Most creators consume content passively. This method forces active deconstruction. Instead of "that was a good video," you extract: why did it stop the scroll, what structure carried it, what emotional trigger did it activate, and what would happen if you ran the same mechanics through your own voice and audience.

Seven creators gives you enough signal to separate universal patterns from individual quirks. Seven posts per creator gives you enough data to see what's consistent versus what was a one-time spike.

---

## What Counts as a Strong Creator to Study

A creator is worth studying if they meet at least 3 of these:

- Their audience overlaps with or is adjacent to yours (ambitious, introspective, 23–35, career/growth/lifestyle)
- They have at least one post that significantly overperformed their average (outlier signal)
- They use formats or hooks you haven't tried yet
- Their content quality exceeds their follower count — underrated signal = strong mechanics
- They operate in a related but not identical niche: enough overlap to be relevant, enough difference to offer new angles

**Do not study creators just because they're large.** A 6K-follower creator with a 53K-share post teaches you more than a 1M creator with predictable content.

---

## What Counts as an Outlier Post

An outlier post is one that significantly exceeded the creator's baseline performance on at least one metric:

- Views 3x+ their average
- Share rate notably higher than their other content
- Comment volume spike
- Heavy saves/bookmarks (if visible)
- Reached people outside their existing audience

**Priority:** High shares > high saves > high views. Views measure reach. Shares measure resonance. Resonance is what you're building toward.

---

## What to Extract from Each Post

**Identity fields**
- Creator name and handle
- Follower count at time of analysis
- Link, views, shares

**Hook fields**
- Spoken hook (first 1–2 sentences said aloud)
- Text hook (on-screen text in the first 2 seconds)
- Visual hook (what the viewer sees in the first frame)

**Format fields**
- Format type (talking head / silent B-roll / relatable comedy / vlog / series / skit / lip sync / reaction)
- Intensity (low / medium / high)
- Duration in seconds
- Audio (track or type)

**Funnel fields**
- Funnel stage: Attract / Nurture / Position / Convert
- CTA (exact text or "none")
- Content pillar mapped to your brand: Skill / Mindset / Lifestyle / Passions

**Analysis fields**
- Why it worked — mechanism, not description
- What you can adapt — specific angles through your creator vision

---

## What the Method Produces

After completing a 7x7 cycle, you should be able to build or update:

1. **Hook library** — categorized patterns with real examples and brand-adapted versions
2. **Format library** — format mechanics, when to use each, what makes each work
3. **Storytelling structure bank** — reusable structures with your own angles mapped in
4. **Emotional trigger map** — which triggers are working in your niche right now
5. **Content idea list** — specific, testable ideas grounded in observation, adapted to your voice
6. **CTA pattern notes** — what CTAs work and in what funnel contexts

These outputs live in `/research/` and get referenced when scripting.
