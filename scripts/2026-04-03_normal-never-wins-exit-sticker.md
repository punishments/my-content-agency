## Script

There's a butterfly conservatory in Key West that does something most brands would never think to do. They stamp you on the way out.

As you're leaving, one of their staff presses a 3D butterfly sticker onto your jacket. Your bag. Your sleeve. Just — here, take this with you.

And you do. And then you tell someone about it. Because how could you not.

Most brands spend everything competing for attention at the entrance. The ad. The first impression. The hook that gets you in the door. The exit? Nobody thinks about the exit.

But here's what that conservatory understood: the last thing someone experiences is the thing they carry. Not just physically. Into every conversation they have after they leave.

Zero ad spend. The guests do the rest.

The exit moment is the most underused touchpoint in marketing — and almost nobody is thinking about it.

So the question is: what's your exit sticker? What do people leave your content carrying?

None — the question is the close. Open loop. Let it sit.

## Production Notes

- **Duration target:** 60–70 seconds
- **Camera:** Talking head, direct to camera — confident, conversational. This is the Position slot: speak with authority, not performance.
- **Delivery:** Tell the story first. The butterfly sticker moment should feel like you're recounting something you genuinely found fascinating. Then shift into the insight. The application question at the end lands with a pause before it.
- **Overlays:** Optional — could flash "Zero ad spend." as a text overlay. Otherwise keep clean.
- **Caption:** Use the reframe hook as caption opener: "Everyone fights for attention at the entrance. The Key West Butterfly Conservatory won at the exit."
- **Series note:** This is Episode 1 of Normal Never Wins — establish the format here. Future episodes follow: unusual stunt → extract insight → application for creators/brands.
