# Running a Full Pipeline as One Person

**Date:** 2026-04-02
**Mission:** Nurture
**Format:** Voiceover
**Intensity:** Low
**Pillar:** Skill / Lifestyle
**Proposal:** `planning/proposals/2026-04-02_full-pipeline_proposal.md`

---

## Hook (Spoken — VO opens)

> 🧠: I can't afford to hire anyone. But I also can't afford to keep doing this without a system.

---

## Voiceover Script

I run a full content pipeline by myself. And I want to show you what that actually looks like — not the clean version.

The pipeline has seven roles. Creative Strategist. Content Strategist. Content Producer. Creative Director. Scriptwriter. QA Editor. Marketing Director.

Seven roles. One person.

I can't afford a team right now. And I found out fast that trying to hold all of those roles in my head at once wasn't working. I'd get stuck. Skip steps. Produce things I wasn't proud of.

So I built it in Claude. Each role is a defined mode. I move through them deliberately. It doesn't think for me — it gives the process a shape I can actually follow.

Is it perfect? No. There are parts I'm still figuring out. The QA step — I still miss things.

But I show up to a system now instead of a blank page.

And that's the difference.

---

## CTA

None — connection is the value.

## Production Notes

- **Visual direction:** B-roll of working — desk setup, laptop screen open to Claude or a doc, typing hands, notebook, coffee. Slow cuts. Nothing polished. The visuals should feel like you're actually in it, not performing productivity.
- **Delivery:** Intimate, slightly unpolished — like thinking aloud. Not a tutorial. No authority posture.
- **Duration target:** 55–65 seconds
- **Music:** Low ambient only — VO must be clearly audible. No music that competes with voice.
- **Edit note:** Let pauses breathe. Don't over-tighten. The hesitation before "And that's the difference" is intentional.
- **Caption:** Short — echo the hook. Something like: "Seven roles. One person. Here's how I actually do it."
