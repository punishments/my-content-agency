# Format Library

Reusable format frameworks extracted from 7x7 research. Each format includes when to use it, mechanics, and adaptation guidance for your brand.

---

## 1. Talking Head

**Creators using it:** npcfaizan, @aimann.ai, natalie.hoffie, @renxriley, harrietjanebeal, @rosehoneymorgan

**Typical duration:** 30s–130s (sweet spot: 45–75s)
**Intensity:** Low to high depending on topic
**Best missions:** Position, Attract, Nurture

---

### When to use it
- You have a structured point to make and you're the proof
- You want to teach, reframe, or share a perspective that requires trust
- The audience needs to see your face to believe the vulnerability

### Mechanics (what makes talking head work in this dataset)
- **Cuts every 2–4 seconds** — not to change the idea, but to create visual movement. Monotony is the retention killer, not length.
- **Physical repositioning** — npcfaizan moves left/right/forward with each cut. This isn't a style choice — it's a retention mechanism. The viewer's eye resets.
- **Closed captions always** — every high-performing talking head has them. Not optional.
- **Camera framing changes** — zoom in for personal moments, zoom out for context. This mimics natural conversation distance.
- **Duration consistency** — npcfaizan's videos are consistently 62–78s. @aimann.ai's are consistently 14–30s. Consistent duration trains the audience's attention contract.

### Intensity settings
| Intensity | What it looks like | Example |
|-----------|-------------------|---------|
| Low | Calm, measured, close | @aimann.ai CEO clips |
| Medium | Engaged, structured, with cuts | npcfaizan positioning videos |
| High | Emotional, faster cadence | @renxriley vision board video |

### Talking head adaptation for your brand
- Use for Position content: frameworks, career reframes, communication breakdowns
- Use for Attract: personal turning points, vulnerability moments, "here's what changed"
- Vary your physical position in frame to signal emotional register (close = intimate, wide = contextual)
- Let the hook carry the watch time — if the first 3 seconds aren't specific enough, the rest doesn't matter

## 2. Silent Film / B-Roll

**Creators using it:** @ayat.kanaan (exclusively), natalie.hoffie, @renxriley, harrietjanebeal

**Typical duration:** 4–15s (rarely longer)
**Intensity:** Low
**Best missions:** Nurture, Attract

---

### When to use it
- The feeling is the message — no words needed
- You want the viewer to project themselves into your content
- You're showing a moment, not explaining a concept
- You want maximum repost and share potential (lowest friction to reshare)

### Mechanics
- **Text overlay is the entire script** — it has to land in 1–2 seconds of reading
- **Audio carries the emotion** — choose tracks the audience already has a feeling about (Beyoncé, Ariana Grande, slowed remixes)
- **B-roll must match the text** — not illustrate it, match it. The visual and the text should say the same thing in two different languages.
- **Low exposure** (dark, moody) tends to outperform bright in this format — it signals interiority
- **6–7 second duration** is the @ayat.kanaan formula — long enough to read the text, short enough to replay automatically

### @ayat.kanaan's formula (extracted)
1. One b-roll clip (herself or broader lifestyle footage)
2. One text overlay (identity statement, 5–10 words)
3. Custom audio mix (consistent cadence — likely Capcut template)
4. Low exposure
5. Duration: 6–7s
6. Repeat exactly. Zero variation. The consistency IS the brand.

### Silent film adaptation for your brand
- Use for Nurture: gym moments, aerial silks, travel, reading — one line of text that says what the moment means
- Use for Attract: broader b-roll (not your face) + identity statement that a stranger can claim
- Texts that work: incomplete sentences, declarative identity statements, anti-resolution statements
- Avoid: explaining. If you have to explain, it's a talking head piece, not a silent film.

## 3. Relatable Comedy

**Creators using it:** natalie.hoffie, @renxriley, @rosehoneymorgan, harrietjanebeal

**Typical duration:** 11–57s
**Intensity:** Low to medium
**Best missions:** Nurture, Attract

---

### When to use it
- The setup IS the joke — no punchline needed, just recognition
- You're showcasing a specific absurdity of the shared human experience
- The goal is "this is so me" more than "this taught me something"

### Mechanics
- **The hook is the entire setup** — if the first line isn't funny or recognizable, the rest won't land
- **Body language and expression carry the video** — often zero voiceover needed
- **Short duration** — 11–24s outperforms 57s in this format. The joke lands or it doesn't.
- **"This is so me" comments** are the signal you want — they mean the audience self-selected
- **Cuts follow action** — the camera moves with her, not ahead of her
- **No payoff required** — the relatability IS the payoff

### The natalie.hoffie model
- Identity hook (POV or statement) → body language that matches → viewer recognition → share
- No advice. No CTA. The share IS the CTA.
- "This is so me" comment dominance = the content is working as an audience mirror

### Relatable comedy adaptation for your brand
- Use for the real moments: the moment before the hard conversation you rehearsed 10 times, the meeting that went differently than planned, the overthinking spiral that turned into a plan
- Let the moment speak — don't editorialize
- Best pillar matches: Lifestyle, Passions, Mindset (self-awareness moments)
- Use when you have a real, specific, non-obvious moment — not a generic situation

## 4. Vlog / Multi-Clip Narrative

**Creators using it:** @renxriley (alone but not lonely series)

**Typical duration:** 45–106s
**Intensity:** Medium to high
**Best missions:** Attract, Nurture

---

### When to use it
- You're documenting a real-life process or day — not explaining it, living it
- You want the audience to follow a journey over time (series format)
- The value is experiential, not instructional

### Mechanics
- **Multiple clips create pace** — each clip serves the arc (don't include footage that doesn't advance the story)
- **Rising audio** — music with building tension holds retention through longer duration
- **Visible aloneness** is a theme in high-performing vlogs here — being seen doing something solo creates intimacy
- **Series structure** ("episode 3") drives profile traffic and subscription behavior — each episode is a CTA for the last one
- **CTA feels relational, not promotional** — "we have more dates" vs. "follow for more"

### Vlog adaptation for your brand
- Document the digital nomad journey, gym progression, aerial silks training, or reading process — not as highlight reels but as honest slices
- Build a named series: a title, a consistent format, a clear premise
- The series CTA is the most natural CTA in content — it's not selling, it's continuing

## 5. Series Format

**Creator using it:** @aimann.ai (Day 1–8 CEO series)

**Typical duration:** 14–30s per episode
**Intensity:** Low to medium
**Best missions:** Attract (compounding)

---

### When to use it
- You have a recurring angle that the audience can anticipate
- The topic is broad enough to have multiple entries but specific enough to have a clear premise
- You want compounding views — each episode pulls traffic to the others

### Why this is the highest-performing format in the dataset
- @aimann.ai's top 6 posts by views are all from the same series
- The hook includes the episode number — viewers who missed earlier episodes seek them out
- Each CTA is the next episode — zero friction, zero ambiguity
- The series creates a completionist impulse (viewers want to see all entries)

### Series mechanics
- **Consistent hook formula** with variable insert: "Day [N] of [premise]"
- **Consistent visual format** — same location, same framing, same energy every episode
- **Sequential CTA** — "follow for Day [N+1]" — the CTA is always the next piece of content
- **No need to be perfect** — consistency across episodes matters more than any single episode

### Series format adaptation for your brand
- Possible series angles:
  - "Day [N] of things my manager said that I cannot stop thinking about"
  - "[N] things I learned navigating a career transition that no one tells you"
  - "Week [N] of actually executing instead of planning"
  - A named format tied to your 7x7 research: "Creator breakdown [N] — what made it work"
- The series hook should be completable in 3 seconds and repeatable across 7+ episodes before it needs to change

## Format Selection Guide

| You want to... | Use this format |
|---------------|----------------|
| Establish authority on a topic | Talking Head (Position) |
| Create emotional resonance with no words | Silent Film / B-Roll |
| Get "this is so me" recognition | Relatable Comedy |
| Show behind the scenes of your life | Vlog |
| Compound views across multiple posts | Series |
| Mix formats in one video | Skit-style (see harrietjanebeal's TUI content) |

---

## Format Combinations That Work

**Silent film → Talking head follow-up**
Post a 6s silent B-roll that creates a question. Follow it with a talking head that answers it. The first piece earns the attention; the second piece earns the follow.

**Relatable comedy → Position**
Let the audience see themselves in your chaos. Then show them the framework you built to address it. The comedy creates the "in." The Position content creates the authority.

**Series → Convert**
Run 5–6 episodes of a series with no CTA. On episode 6 or 7, introduce the offer. The trust is already there.
